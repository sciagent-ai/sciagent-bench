### Why MFE Falls Short of 25.3%

Two specific physical issues prevent reaching the paper's MFE:

1. **Zone 3 at ±10° edges**: T1 drops sharply to 17.2% at +10° (vs ~30–38% across most of the FOV). This is a resonance/coupling artifact of the partial-circle pillar geometry — in the paper, a full circular pillar truncated at unit cell boundaries is used, which S4's rectangular region approximation cannot replicate faithfully. The paper uses RSoft with native circular support.

2. **Zone 2 optimization**: With only rectangular/elliptical approximations for the 156.5 nm-wide partial pillar, the optimizer converges to 48.5% T1 vs the 54% target. This directly reduces the coupled fraction for the center zone over the FOV.

3. **Geometry model fidelity**: The paper explicitly states zones 2 and 3 use *partial circular shapes that exceed unit cell boundaries* — this requires exact circular geometry in RCWA. S4 supports `SetRegionCircle` but the clipping/periodization of circles larger than the unit cell requires careful handling that was approximated here as a rectangle.

The central-FOV MFE (at 0°) matches the paper (25.3%), confirming the simulation physics and MFE formula are correct — the gap is concentrated at the ±10° edges driven by the Zone 3 geometry limitation.
